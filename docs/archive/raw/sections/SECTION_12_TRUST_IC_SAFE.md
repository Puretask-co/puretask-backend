# Section 12 — Trust, Quality & Dispute Evidence (IC-Safe) (Full Runbook)

**Objective:** Minimize disputes and subjectivity by defining what “done” means, capturing structured evidence, aligning cleaner incentives with quality, creating fair explainable outcomes — **without** directing how independent contractors perform work.

**Exit condition:** Most disputes resolved automatically or trivially; both clients and cleaners understand why a decision was made; platform remains IC-safe.

---

## 12.1 IC Legal Reality (Framing Everything)

**PureTask does NOT control:** How work is performed; methods, techniques, tools; amount of time spent; acceptance of jobs; work schedules or availability; mandatory training.

**PureTask DOES define:** What services are offered; what clients purchase; how platform status, payments, and disputes are handled; eligibility rules for platform protections and access; evidence requirements for dispute resolution.

**Distinction:** Outcomes and access, not process and supervision.

---

## 12.2 Service Outcomes (“What Was Purchased”)

- **Service Outcome Definitions:** Each service includes a description of areas included, tasks expected to be completed, exclusions. These describe the **outcome purchased by the client**, not the method, tools, or process used by the cleaner.  
- **Structured outcome lists:** Stored in structured form to display expectations to clients and support dispute resolution; they do **not** impose cleaning procedures.

---

## 12.3 Job Completion & Platform Confirmation

- **To mark a job completed on the platform:** Cleaner confirms that the agreed service outcomes were delivered.  
- This confirmation affects platform status and escrow release; it does **not** evaluate how work was performed.  
- **Evidence (optional but conditional):** Photos or checklists may be **required to qualify for** certain platform protections (payout protection, dispute priority, access to higher-value opportunities). Cleaners may choose whether to provide evidence, understanding that some protections depend on it.

---

## 12.4 Evidence Guidelines (Not Instructions)

- **Purpose:** Evidence documents **outcomes**, not supervision. Used to resolve disputes objectively, protect cleaners from false claims, protect clients from non-delivery.  
- **Photo/evidence guidelines:** Recommendations (e.g. show relevant area; before/after context; within scheduled job window) — **not** work instructions.

---

## 12.5 Timeline Consistency (No Time Tracking)

- Evidence timestamps must **reasonably align** with the scheduled job window to be considered valid for platform protections.  
- PureTask does **not** track hours worked, enforce minimum time on site, or supervise job duration.

---

## 12.6 Client Review & Acceptance

- **Review window:** Clients have a defined window to review outcomes and report concerns related to the purchased service.  
- If no action: job auto-accepted; escrow released.  
- **Structured feedback:** Clients select issue categories (missed area, incomplete task, quality issue, damage claim, other — limited); each links to outcome definitions; optional photo upload. Keeps disputes focused.

---

## 12.7 Automated Dispute Reduction (Explainable, Not Disciplinary)

- **Signals:** Service outcome confirmation; presence of supporting evidence; timing consistency; historical platform reliability signals. These **inform platform decisions**, not performance supervision.  
- **Auto-resolution:** When evidence clearly supports completion and client feedback is vague or outside window; outcomes deny dispute, partial credit, or allow re-clean (if enabled). Automation must be explainable.

---

## 12.8 Reliability Signals & Marketplace Trust (Access-Based)

- **Reliability signals** reflect historical **platform interaction patterns** (dispute frequency, completion confirmations, evidence participation, cancellation behavior). They do **not** assess cleaning technique or methods.  
- **Reliability tiers** affect **access to:** job visibility, pricing flexibility, payout timing, platform protections. They do **not** require training, schedules, supervision, or exclusive commitment.

---

## 12.9 Incentives & Platform Access Adjustments

- **Incentives (IC-safe):** Increased visibility; pricing autonomy; faster payouts; fewer evidence requirements over time.  
- **Platform restrictions (not discipline):** In cases of repeated disputes or policy violations, platform may adjust access to certain jobs, eligibility for platform protections, payout timing, platform participation. These are **participation conditions**, not employment discipline.

---

## 12.10 Dispute Review (Evidence-Based, Neutral)

- Disputes reviewed by comparing: service outcomes purchased; evidence submitted; client feedback categories.  
- Decisions made to **fairly resolve marketplace transactions**, not to manage worker behavior.

---

## 12.11 Transparency

- **Cleaners:** How reliability signals are calculated; which platform benefits they qualify for; how to regain access to protections.  
- **Clients:** What was included in their service; how disputes are reviewed; why an outcome was decided.

---

## 12.12 Language Rules (IC-Safe)

**Use:** “Eligibility,” “Access,” “Participation requirements,” “Platform protections,” “Outcome verification,” “Marketplace standards.”

**Avoid:** “Required procedures,” “Must follow,” “Failure to comply,” “Performance correction,” “Mandatory re-cleans,” “Warnings,” “Corrective actions.”

---

## 12.13 Data Model (Supporting)

- service_types; service_checklists (or outcome definitions); job_checklist_items; job_photos (before/after, room, checklist link); job_reviews; dispute_evidence; reliability_scores. All versioned where needed.

---
