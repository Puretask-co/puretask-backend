# TOS Clause Rationale for Counsel Review

**Purpose:** Rationale for each TOS clause to support legal review. (Indexed as LAWYER_REVIEW_NOTES in README.)  
**Last updated:** 2026-02-02

---

## Clause 1 — Platform Role

**Why:** Establishes that PureTask is a technology/marketplace platform, not an employer or cleaning provider. Clarifies the tri-party relationship: PureTask (platform), client (purchaser), cleaner (service provider).

---

## Clause 2 — Independent Contractor Relationship

**Why:** Explicit IC designation. Mitigates misclassification risk. Reinforces that cleaners are responsible for taxes, insurance, tools; PureTask does not withhold or provide benefits.

---

## Clause 3 — No Control Over Performance

**Why:** Directly addresses ABC Test Prong A (control). States that PureTask does not prescribe methods, techniques, tools, or time. Critical for California AB5 and similar laws.

---

## Clause 4 — No Exclusivity

**Why:** Supports ABC Test Prong C (independently established trade). Cleaners may work elsewhere. No minimum hours or quotas.

---

## Clause 5 — Service Descriptions & Outcomes

**Why:** Distinguishes "outcomes" (what clients purchase) from "methods" (how cleaners work). Avoids language that could imply direction of work. Aligns with IC-safe framing.

---

## Clause 6 — Evidence & Verification

**Why:** Evidence is framed as outcome verification and dispute resolution, not supervision. Optional for basic participation; required only for certain protections. Avoids "mandatory procedure" language that could imply control.

---

## Clause 7 — Job Completion & Payments

**Why:** Describes escrow, confirmation, and payout flow. Clarifies platform fee. No language implying wage or employment relationship.

---

## Clause 8 — Client Review & Disputes

**Why:** Defines dispute process as marketplace resolution, not employment discipline. Outcomes (refund, no refund) are transaction-focused. Avoids "performance correction" or "disciplinary" language.

---

## Clause 9 — Platform Trust Signals & Access

**Why:** Reliability/trust signals affect access (visibility, payout timing), not employment status. Explicitly states these are participation conditions, not discipline. Critical for IC-safe admin and dispute handling.

---

## Clause 10 — Administrative Actions

**Why:** Allows platform to suspend, hold payouts, adjust credits. Uses IC-safe terms ("platform status adjustment," "risk indicators"). Audit and appeal paths documented. Avoids employment-termination framing.

---

## Clause 11 — Reservation of Rights

**Why:** Standard reservation to modify Terms and policies. Protects platform flexibility while requiring notice for material changes.
