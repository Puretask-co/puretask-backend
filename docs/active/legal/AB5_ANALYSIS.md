# California AB5 / ABC Test — Prong-by-Prong Memo

**Purpose:** Memo for counsel on California AB5 (ABC Test) compliance.  
**Last updated:** 2026-02-02

---

## Background

California Assembly Bill 5 (AB5) codifies the ABC Test for determining whether a worker is an employee or independent contractor. A worker is presumed to be an employee unless the hiring entity proves all three prongs of the ABC Test.

---

## Prong A: Control

**Test:** The worker is free from the control and direction of the hiring entity in connection with the performance of the work, both under the contract for the performance of the work and in fact.

**PureTask design:**

- PureTask does not control how cleaners perform work. No prescribed methods, techniques, tools, or time on site.
- Cleaners choose when to accept jobs and their availability.
- Service descriptions define outcomes purchased by clients, not procedures for cleaners.
- Evidence requirements are outcome-verification, not supervision. Evidence is optional for basic participation; required only to qualify for certain protections.
- Reliability signals affect access (visibility, payout timing), not work performance.

**Conclusion:** Prong A satisfied — cleaners are free from PureTask’s control in performing work.

---

## Prong B: Outside Usual Course

**Test:** The worker performs work that is outside the usual course of the hiring entity’s business.

**PureTask design:**

- PureTask’s business is operating a technology platform: software, payments, dispute resolution, marketplace infrastructure.
- Cleaning services are performed by independent contractors. PureTask does not provide cleaning services; it facilitates connections between clients and cleaners.
- PureTask is analogous to a marketplace or referral platform, not a cleaning company.

**Conclusion:** Prong B satisfied — cleaning work is outside the usual course of PureTask’s platform business.

---

## Prong C: Independently Established Trade

**Test:** The worker is customarily engaged in an independently established trade, occupation, or business of the same nature as that involved in the work performed.

**PureTask design:**

- Cleaners may work for other platforms and clients. No exclusivity.
- Cleaners are responsible for their own taxes, insurance, tools.
- Cleaners have discretion over acceptance, availability, and methods.
- The platform does not prohibit cleaners from advertising, having other clients, or operating as an independent cleaning business.

**Conclusion:** Prong C satisfied — cleaners are engaged in an independently established trade.

---

## Summary

All three prongs of the ABC Test are satisfied by the current design. This memo is for counsel review; jurisdiction-specific advice should be obtained before reliance.
