# Independent Contractor Safeguards — Appendix for TOS & In-App

**Purpose:** Full text for TOS appendix and in-app display. Ensures IC-safe framing.  
**Last updated:** 2026-02-02

---

## What PureTask Does NOT Control

- How work is performed (methods, techniques, tools)
- Amount of time spent on a job
- Acceptance of jobs or work schedules
- Worker availability or exclusivity
- Mandatory training or procedures
- Performance supervision or evaluation of work methods

---

## What PureTask DOES Define

- What services are offered and what clients purchase
- How platform status, payments, and disputes are handled
- Eligibility rules for platform protections and access
- Evidence requirements for dispute resolution
- Marketplace standards for participation

---

## Evidence & Verification

Evidence (photos, checklists) documents **outcomes**, not supervision. It is used to:

- Resolve disputes objectively
- Protect cleaners from false claims
- Protect clients from non-delivery
- Support eligibility for platform protections

Submission may be required to qualify for certain protections. Cleaners choose whether to provide evidence, understanding the trade-off. Guidelines describe what evidence is useful (e.g., before/after context, relevant areas); they are **not** work instructions.

---

## Platform Access

Reliability and trust signals affect **access** to:

- Job visibility
- Payout timing
- Eligibility for platform protections
- Platform participation

These signals reflect historical platform interaction patterns. They do **not** assess cleaning technique or impose employment-like supervision. Adjustments to access are participation conditions, not discipline.

---

## No Exclusivity

Cleaners may work for other platforms and clients. PureTask does not require exclusivity. Cleaners accept or decline jobs at their discretion.

---

## Final Statement

Cleaners are independent contractors. PureTask facilitates a marketplace; it does not employ, supervise, or direct how work is performed. The platform defines outcomes and access; it does not control methods or mandate procedures.
