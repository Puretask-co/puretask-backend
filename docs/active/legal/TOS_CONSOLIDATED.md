# PureTask Terms of Service — Consolidated Draft

**Status:** Draft for counsel review. Merge of all TOS sections from the PureTask hardening program.  
**Last updated:** 2026-02-02

---

## 1. Platform Role

PureTask operates a technology platform that connects clients seeking cleaning services with independent contractors who provide those services. PureTask does not employ cleaners. PureTask provides software, payment processing, dispute resolution, and marketplace infrastructure. Clients contract directly with cleaners for the performance of services; PureTask facilitates that connection and related transactions.

---

## 2. Independent Contractor Relationship

Cleaners are independent contractors, not employees or agents of PureTask. Cleaners are responsible for their own taxes, insurance, tools, and methods. Cleaners have discretion over how they perform work, when they accept jobs, and their availability. Nothing in these Terms creates an employment, agency, or partnership relationship between PureTask and any cleaner.

---

## 3. No Control Over Performance

PureTask does not control how cleaners perform work. PureTask does not prescribe methods, techniques, tools, or time on site. Cleaners retain full discretion over the manner and means of performing services. PureTask defines what services are offered and what outcomes clients purchase; it does not direct how those outcomes are achieved.

---

## 4. No Exclusivity

Cleaners may work for other platforms and clients. PureTask does not require exclusivity. Cleaners may accept or decline jobs at their discretion. No minimum hours, quotas, or availability requirements are imposed by PureTask.

---

## 5. Service Descriptions & Outcomes

PureTask publishes service descriptions that define what clients purchase: areas included, tasks expected, and exclusions. These describe the **outcome** purchased by the client, not the method or process used by the cleaner. Service outcomes are used to set expectations and support dispute resolution; they do not impose cleaning procedures on independent contractors.

---

## 6. Evidence & Verification

Clients and cleaners may submit photos or other evidence related to jobs. Evidence is used to verify service delivery and resolve disputes. Submission of evidence may be **required to qualify for** certain platform protections (e.g., payout protection, dispute priority). Cleaners may choose whether to provide evidence, understanding that some protections depend on it. Evidence documents outcomes, not supervision.

---

## 7. Job Completion & Payments

Clients prepay for services via the platform. Funds are held in escrow until job completion. Cleaners confirm when agreed service outcomes have been delivered. Upon confirmation (or auto-accept after the review window), escrow is released to the cleaner minus the platform fee. Payouts are processed per the platform’s payout schedule. PureTask collects a platform fee as disclosed at the time of booking.

---

## 8. Client Review & Disputes

Clients have a defined window to review completed work and report concerns. If no action is taken, the job is auto-accepted and escrow is released. Clients may file disputes within the dispute window (e.g., 48 hours of completion). Disputes are reviewed by comparing service outcomes, evidence, and client feedback. Decisions are made to fairly resolve marketplace transactions. Resolution options include refund (full or partial), no refund, or other outcomes as defined by platform policy.

---

## 9. Platform Trust Signals & Access

PureTask may calculate reliability or trust signals based on historical platform interaction patterns (completion confirmations, evidence participation, dispute frequency, cancellation behavior). These signals affect **access** to platform features: job visibility, payout timing, eligibility for platform protections. They do not assess cleaning technique or impose employment-like supervision. PureTask may adjust access based on participation conditions; such adjustments are marketplace standards, not employment discipline.

---

## 10. Administrative Actions

PureTask may take administrative actions to maintain marketplace integrity: suspend or terminate platform access, hold payouts during disputes, adjust credits per policy, or take other actions permitted by these Terms. Such actions are documented and subject to appeal as defined in platform policy. Language used is IC-safe: “platform status adjustment,” “risk indicators,” and “participation conditions,” not employment discipline terms.

---

## 11. Reservation of Rights

PureTask reserves the right to modify these Terms, service offerings, and policies with notice. Continued use of the platform constitutes acceptance. PureTask reserves the right to refuse service, suspend accounts, or take other actions necessary to protect the platform and users.
